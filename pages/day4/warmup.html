Why today's warm-up is different

Today you're building a photo booth that takes a lot of pictures. That raises two new problems: how do you keep a growing list of filter options organized in code, and how do you know when your SD card is running out of room. Both problems have a clean tool.

1Lists

A list holds many values in one variable, in order.

filters = ["Grayscale", "Sepia", "Normal"]

for name in filters:
    print(name)

2Dictionaries

A dictionary pairs up a label with a value, a name with a function, a word with a definition, anything. Today you'll pair a filter's name with the actual function that applies it.

ages = {"Sam": 12, "Priya": 13}
print(ages["Sam"])

for name, age in ages.items():
    print(name, "is", age)

4Checking disk space

A photo booth fills up storage fast. Two commands to keep an eye on it.

CommandWhat it does
df -hShows how full the whole SD card is
du -sh foldernameShows the total size of one folder
Your teacher spent this week wrestling with SD cards. This is exactly the kind of command that catches a full card before it becomes a bigger problem.
