Before You Start

Getting Started with Raspberry Pi

Everything used across this camp, what to buy, what to read, and where to keep learning after the week is over.

What You'll Need

Every one of these gets used somewhere across the five days. Quantities assume one full kit per student.

Raspberry Pi 3B+
The board itself. This camp is built specifically for this model.
microSD card, 16GB+
Flashed with Raspberry Pi OS. Buy a couple of spares, they get corrupted more often than you'd like.
5V/2.5A power supply
Official or a reliable equivalent. Underpowered supplies cause flaky, hard-to-diagnose bugs.
Monitor + HDMI cable
The 3B+ uses a full-size HDMI port, no adapter needed.
USB keyboard & mouse
Any standard USB (or wireless USB dongle) set works.
Breadboard
A standard half-size or full-size breadboard per student.
Jumper wires
A mixed pack of male-to-male and male-to-female wires.
Resistors, 220–330Ω
For protecting LEDs. A small assortment pack covers this easily.
LEDs, assorted colors
At least 3 per student for the Day 2 GUI project.
Push buttons
At least 3 per student across the week's projects.
Active buzzer
Used for volume control and the parking sensor project.
Ultrasonic sensor (HC-SR04)
Trigger and echo pins, used for the whole distance-sensing unit.
Pi Camera Module
Standard 15-pin CSI connector, compatible with the 3B+ (v1, v2, or the standard-connector version of Module 3).
1602 I2C LCD screen
A GeeekPi or equivalent 16x2 character display with an I2C backpack.

Book Recommendations

Ordered roughly by how closely they match this camp's age range and topics.

  • Raspberry Pi Projects for Kids — Dan Aldred
    No Starch Press. Explicitly built around the Pi 3B+, covers LEDs, the camera, and sensors, closely mirrors this camp's project arc.
    ↗
  • Adventures in Raspberry Pi, 3rd Edition — Carrie Anne Philbin
    Written specifically for ages 11–15, project-based, from a former Director of Education at the Raspberry Pi Foundation.
    ↗
  • Hello Raspberry Pi! — Ryan Heitz
    Manning Publications. A gentler on-ramp for a first-time programmer, good pick for a student who wants extra practice before or after camp.
    ↗
  • The Official Raspberry Pi Beginner's Guide
    Straight from the Raspberry Pi Foundation. The most comprehensive hardware reference of the bunch, available in print or as a PDF/ePub.
    ↗
